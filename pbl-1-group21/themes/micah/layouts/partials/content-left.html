Flint Michigan Heavy Metal Water Crisis

Due to the popularity and longevity of lead plumbing, there have been several notable (and many more less publicized) instances of lead contamination in the 21st century. Of today’s water crisis, the Flint Water Crisis is probably the most popular and best documented, due to its popularity. The Flint Water Crisis was a 4 year long water crisis, during which primarily lead, but also other contaminants such as legionnaires disease, were prevalent in the Flint Water System due to poor management and not following industry standard water safety practices. The direct cause of the Flint Water Crisis was almost certainly poverty due to the GM downsizings in the 1980’s (click on the graph to the left), leading to a slow and painful economic decline in Flint, ending with the state of Michigan taking over Flint’s finances and making the decision to switch water sources. Had Flint been a prosperous city, the lead service pipes would have either been replaced or properly maintained and the city of Flint wouldn’t have been placed into the hands of a state appointed emergency manager, who lead the city so poorly. To conclude, the Flint Water Crisis was almost entirely due to poverty, as without poverty, Flint would not have had to switch its water source from the Detroit Water System to the Flint River.
